My Time at UCLA

Reflections on growth, learning, and leadership.

During my time at the University of California, Los Angeles, I immersed myself in a vibrant academic and social community that challenged and inspired me. As a member of Kappa Alpha Theta, I participated in service initiatives, supported philanthropic efforts for National CASA/GAL, and built meaningful connections with students from diverse backgrounds.

UCLA’s academic environment pushed me to think critically and communicate clearly. I developed a deeper understanding of social issues affecting children and families through service work, and I was involved in organizing events and securing sponsorships for fundraising campaigns. These experiences sharpened my leadership skills and taught me how to manage real-world projects with empathy and strategic planning.

My time on campus helped shape the person I am today—curious, committed, and community-oriented. It laid a strong foundation for my continued academic and professional journey in the business world.
